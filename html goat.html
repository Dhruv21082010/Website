What is Ayurveda?

Ayurveda is an ancient Indian system of natural healing that dates back over 5,000 years. The word Ayurveda means "science of life" (Ayur = Life, Veda = Knowledge).




Core Beliefs

Ayurveda teaches that health is achieved by maintaining a balance between the body, mind, and spirit. It is based on five elements: space, air, fire, water, and earth.




The Three Doshas

These elements combine to form three life forces or energies called Doshas:

  • Vata(Air + Space) – Controls movement and flexibility
  • Pitta(Fire + Water) – Controls digestion and metabolism
  • Kapha(Earth + Water) – Controls structure and immunity

Each person has a unique combination of these doshas, which defines their Ayurvedic constitution.




Practices

  • Personalized diet based on dosha
  • Daily routine (Dinacharya) and seasonal routines (Ritucharya)
  • Herbal remedies like Ashwagandha, Tulsi, Triphala
  • Yoga, meditation, and breathing exercises
  • Detox therapies like Panchakarma



Goal of Ayurveda

The aim is to prevent illness, promote longevity, and restore balance using natural and holistic methods.
